## 1. Legal Framework

### Care Act 2014
- **Full Text**: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2014/23/contents
- **Key Sections**:
  - Section 14: Assessment of needs
  - Section 17: Charging and financial assessment
  - Section 18: Duty to meet needs
- **Status**: Primary legislation - all Local Authorities must comply
- **Your Use**: Base legal framework for all funding decisions

### Care Act Regulations 2014
- **Full Text**: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2014/2672/contents
- **Key Regulations**:
  - Regulation 14: Income disregards (DLA Mobility, War Disablement Pension)
  - Regulation 15: Attendance Allowance disregard
  - Regulation 20: Property disregards
  - Regulation 21: Tariff income calculation
- **Status**: Mandatory - all LAs must apply these rules
- **Your Use**: Specific rules for income/asset disregards

### LAC Circulars (Local Authority Circulars)
- **Current Thresholds (2024-2025)**:
  - Upper capital limit: £23,250 (LAC(DHSC)(2025)1)
  - Lower capital limit: £14,250
  - Tariff income rate: £1 per £250 of capital above lower limit
- **Source**: Department of Health and Social Care
- **URL**: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications
- **Status**: Updated annually
- **Your Use**: Current financial thresholds for means test

## 3. Means Test Thresholds (2024-2025)

### Capital Limits
- **Upper Limit**: £23,250
  - Source: LAC(DHSC)(2025)1
  - Above this: Full self-funding required
  - Below this: Means-tested support available
  
- **Lower Limit**: £14,250
  - Source: LAC(DHSC)(2025)1
  - Below this: Maximum LA support (income disregarded)
  - Between limits: Tariff income applies

### Tariff Income
- **Rate**: £1 per £250 of capital above lower limit
- **Calculation**: (Capital - £14,250) / 250
- **Source**: Care Act Regulations 2014, Regulation 21
- **Example**: £20,000 capital = (£20,000 - £14,250) / 250 = £23/week tariff income

### Income Assessment
- **Assessable Income**: Total income minus disregards
- **Minimum Income Guarantee (MIG)**: Protected income level
- **Source**: Care Act 2014, Section 17
- **Your Use**: Calculation of weekly contribution

## 4. Income Disregards

### Fully Disregarded Income (100%)

#### DLA Mobility Component
- **Regulation**: Care Act Regulations 2014, Regulation 14(1)(a)
- **Status**: Mandatory - all LAs must disregard
- **URL**: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2014/2672/regulation/14
- **Your Use**: If LA counted this, appeal citing Regulation 14

#### War Disablement Pension
- **Regulation**: Care Act Regulations 2014, Regulation 14(1)(b)
- **Status**: Mandatory disregard
- **URL**: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2014/2672/regulation/14
- **Your Use**: Appeal if LA included in means test

#### PIP Mobility Component
- **Regulation**: Care Act Regulations 2014, Regulation 14(1)(c)
- **Status**: Mandatory disregard
- **Your Use**: Verify LA has disregarded this

#### Attendance Allowance
- **Regulation**: Care Act Regulations 2014, Regulation 15
- **Status**: Mandatory disregard if in care home
- **URL**: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2014/2672/regulation/15
- **Your Use**: Check if LA correctly disregarded

### Partially Disregarded Income

#### Savings Credit
- **Disregard**: 60% disregarded
- **Regulation**: Care Act Regulations 2014, Regulation 14(2)
- **Status**: Mandatory partial disregard
- **Your Use**: Verify correct percentage applied

### Disability-Related Expenditure (DRE)
- **Regulation**: Care Act 2014, Section 17(9)
- **Status**: Discretionary but should be considered
- **Examples**: 
  - Extra heating costs
  - Special equipment
  - Additional transport
  - Specialist clothing
- **Your Use**: Claim DRE to reduce assessable income

## 5. Asset Disregards

### Mandatory Disregards

#### Personal Possessions
- **Status**: Fully disregarded
- **Source**: Care Act 2014 guidance
- **Your Use**: Verify LA has not counted personal items

#### Life Insurance Policies
- **Status**: Fully disregarded if no surrender value
- **Source**: Care Act guidance
- **Your Use**: Check if policy has surrender value

#### Investment Bonds (Life Insurance)
- **Status**: Disregarded if life insurance element
- **Source**: Care Act guidance
- **Your Use**: Verify correct treatment

#### Personal Injury Trusts
- **Status**: Fully disregarded
- **Source**: Care Act 2014, Section 17
- **Your Use**: Trust assets should not be counted

#### Personal Injury Compensation
- **Status**: Temporary disregard (52 weeks)
- **Source**: Care Act guidance
- **Your Use**: Time-limited protection

#### Infected Blood Compensation
- **Status**: Fully disregarded
- **Source**: Government scheme
- **Your Use**: Verify LA has disregarded

#### Business Assets
- **Status**: Disregarded if actively used in business
- **Source**: Care Act guidance
- **Your Use**: Check business use criteria

### Property Disregards

#### Main Residence (12-Week Disregard)
- **Duration**: 12 weeks from entering care
- **Regulation**: Care Act Regulations 2014, Regulation 20
- **Status**: Mandatory temporary disregard
- **URL**: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2014/2672/regulation/20
- **Your Use**: Verify 12-week period correctly applied

#### Partner Residing in Property
- **Status**: Permanent disregard
- **Regulation**: Care Act Regulations 2014, Regulation 20(1)(a)
- **Your Use**: Property should be disregarded if partner lives there

#### Relative 60+ Residing in Property
- **Status**: Permanent disregard
- **Regulation**: Care Act Regulations 2014, Regulation 20(1)(b)
- **Your Use**: Verify relative's age and residence

#### Incapacitated Relative
- **Status**: Permanent disregard
- **Regulation**: Care Act Regulations 2014, Regulation 20(1)(c)
- **Your Use**: Check incapacity criteria

#### Child Under 18
- **Status**: Permanent disregard
- **Regulation**: Care Act Regulations 2014, Regulation 20(1)(d)
- **Your Use**: Verify child's age and residence

#### Non-Residential Care
- **Status**: Property disregarded if receiving non-residential care
- **Regulation**: Care Act Regulations 2014, Regulation 20(2)
- **Your Use**: Check care type

#### Third Party Occupation
- **Status**: Discretionary disregard
- **Regulation**: Care Act guidance
- **Your Use**: LA may disregard if reasonable

## 7. DPA (Deferred Payment Agreement) Eligibility

### Eligibility Criteria
- **Capital (excluding property)**: Below £23,250
- **Property Value**: Above £23,250 (when included)
- **Qualifying Relative**: Partner, relative 60+, incapacitated relative, child under 18
- **Source**: Care Act 2014, Section 34-36
- **URL**: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2014/23/section/34
- **Your Use**: Check DPA eligibility criteria

### Interest Rates
- **Rate**: Set by LA (typically 0-2.5% above base rate)
- **Source**: Care Act 2014, Section 35
- **Status**: Varies by LA
- **Your Use**: Check LA's DPA interest rate

## 8. Appeal Process

### CHC Appeals
- **Process**: 
  1. Request review within 6 months
  2. Independent Review Panel (IRP)
  3. Ombudsman (if IRP unsuccessful)
- **Time Limits**: 6 months from decision
- **Source**: National Framework for CHC
- **URL**: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/national-framework-for-nhs-continuing-healthcare-and-nhs-funded-nursing-care
- **Your Use**: Appeal guidance with legal citations

### LA Funding Appeals
- **Process**:
  1. Request review within 28 days
  2. Local resolution
  3. Ombudsman (if unresolved)
- **Time Limits**: 28 days from decision
- **Source**: Care Act 2014, Section 72
- **URL**: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2014/23/section/72
- **Your Use**: Appeal procedures with time limits

### How to Use This Document for Appeals
1. **Identify the Issue**: Which calculation is incorrect?
2. **Find the Source**: Use this document to find relevant regulation/section
3. **Cite the Source**: Reference exact regulation number and URL
4. **Provide Evidence**: Show how LA's calculation differs from legal requirement
5. **Request Correction**: Ask LA to recalculate using correct rules

**Example Appeal Letter**:
```
Dear [Council],

I dispute your funding calculation dated [date] because:

1. You included DLA Mobility Component in my assessable income.
   This is incorrect under Care Act Regulations 2014, Regulation 14(1)(a).
   Source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2014/2672/regulation/14

2. You did not apply the 12-week property disregard.
   Under Regulation 20, my property should be disregarded for 12 weeks.
   Source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2014/2672/regulation/20

Please recalculate my contribution using the correct legal framework.

Yours sincerely,
[Your name]
```
